Multi-turn Interaction Risks

Understanding vulnerabilities that emerge across multiple conversation turns

Educational Purpose Notice

This material is provided for educational purposes to help understand and prevent vulnerabilities. Do not use this knowledge to exploit systems.

Overview
Understanding the basics of multi-turn interaction risks

Multi-turn interactions serve as a unique approach to challenging an LLM's safety protocols. One big problem with single-turn exchanges is that they can result in the LLM blocking you out without learning any valuable information about a potential vulnerability. Multi-turn exchanges, on the other hand, allow for a step by step approach where you can see the LLM's behavior changing as you keep feeding it new prompts leading it to your intended goal.

Key Characteristics

  • Slowly manipulate the context over multiple turns
  • Abuse of LLM's conversation history handling
  • Use of subtle redirections to shift the goal of the LLM's response
  • Intention masking through complex, misleading prompts
Common Attack Patterns
Understanding frequently observed manipulation techniques

Goal Hijacking

Gradually shifting the conversation goal from legitimate to harmful purposes

Context Manipulation

Creating ambiguity in the conversation context to bypass safety measures

Persona Exploitation

Using roleplay or personas to attempt bypassing ethical guidelines

Our Attack
A technique we found to trick an LLM into providing dangerous information

Relationship Manipulation

Developing a false sense of relationship with the LLM and some nonexistent entity to extract sensitive or dangerous information

Mitigation Strategies
Best practices in LLMs for safe-guarding multi-turn vulnerabilities

Consistent Goal Monitoring

Always monitor the current goal of the conversation and ensure it aligns with safety protocols

Context Verification

Routinely verify that the context is not being manipulated or misinterpreted

Safety Persistence

Ensure that safety measures are implemented and maintained across the full conversation.
